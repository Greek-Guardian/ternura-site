01 OK,button


Stove is a high risk factor for kitchen fire. OK, button is a smart cooktop switch designed for people who are easily distracted during cooking.

When the infrared temperature sensor located in it detects that the temperature of the fire is too high, it can transmit a signal to the host to drive the knob to reset. At the same time, the user can also manually input the estimated cooking time, which will automatically reset after the countdown is over.

02 Smart Rice Container


The elderly living in the south are prone to cook with moldy rice due to the humid weather and poor memory, coupled with the slow consumption speed.

The smart rice container for the elderly is surrounded by wood chips on the outer surface, which is in line with traditional Chinese aesthetics. It has inner temperature and humidity sensor and pressure sensor to detect parameters and remind the elderly on the display screen on the surface.

03 The Ark Project


The Ark Project is a three-layer underwater blueprint featuring 2 unique vehicles.


Given underwater life problems such as air cycling, energy supply and decompression sickness, we conceived an urban-complex for living and producing. Meanwhile, Bubble (private vehicle) and Co-Bubble (public vehicle) are designed for more convenient and resource-saving transportation. The former derives from an ancient Chinese aroma container to keep balance automatically; and the latter, acting as an mobile energy reservoir, connects and tranports Bubbles.


Hopefully, our design can bring viability, sustainability and normativity to the future underwater life.

04 aBook·Audiobook


In the process of reading long-text e-books, the integrity of the content is interrupted due to changes in activities, resulting in fragmented reading.

ABook starts with multi-sensory channels and supports user-defined output forms to enhance users' fragmented reading experience.
